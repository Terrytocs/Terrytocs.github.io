11/20/2025

I'm making great progress on the first game, pong. I was also able to finish the first project on the site. I was using the dot product for pong collisions but thought that was too expensive for a simple game like this, so I ended up doing collisions based on the positions. I didn't use a state machine for the game and that decision is making it a real pain to deal with, but I'm already too far in to change everything now. I've learned my lesson for the next game. Collisions in general are also a real pain to deal with. I'm going to have to use the S.A.T. with collision info for most game collisions or I'll have to deal with some major inconveniences when it comes to using position based collision. For the clock project I made a 24 hour continuous clock. I did that because something about seeing the 24 hours makes me more aware of how much time I have in a day, and the continuous movement reminds me that time is always moving so I should keep moving as well. For a game as simple as Pong it's kind of surprising the amount of thought that I have to put into it. I can only imagine how it's going to be when I move on to more difficult games. I'm planning to use my own music for some of the games as well, and I'm looking forward to composing some interesting stuff even though I'm really no musician and I have no idea how to mix, but the experience will still be great I think. The good news about Pong is that it's currently in a playable state and now I just need to work on the overall game experience as well as polishing. I may also have a bug or two that I'll need to squash. I'm definitely not where I'd want to be, but I think I've come a really long way in terms of my math skills, at least for simple maths. I even finally found a use for the dot product; althought the more educated among us may have known this since forever I never knew a good use for the dot product but I recently learned that if you use it for a vector and a normal in some direction you get the length of the vetor in the direction of the normal. I'm sure this will come in handy at some point. Although I kind of knew this because I use it for collision resolution, but the concept got cemented with this Pong project.
